Games Done Quick (GDQ) is making waves by hosting its first-ever event in Europe, marking a significant milestone in the charity speedrunning community. This exciting collaboration with Gamescom will feature three days of live speedruns, showcasing top-tier players from around the globe while supporting a noble cause. With a history of raising over $60 million for various charities, GDQ continues to demonstrate the powerful impact of gaming on social good.

The event will take place during the renowned Gamescom expo in Cologne, Germany, and will be streamed live on platforms like Twitch and YouTube. This partnership not only highlights the growing popularity of speedrunning but also emphasizes the importance of civic engagement through gaming, as the proceeds will benefit the Gaming for Democracy initiative. As Ashley Farkas from GDQ noted, this expansion allows more runners to participate and share their passion with a wider audience.

As we look forward to this groundbreaking event, it raises an intriguing question: how will the European gaming community respond to this unique blend of competition and charity? The future of gaming as a force for good is bright, and we cant wait to see how this unfolds at Gamescom.
