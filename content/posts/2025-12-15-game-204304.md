This week, the gaming world witnessed a historic moment as French game Clair Obscur: Expedition 33 swept the prestigious Game Awards, setting a new record with nine wins out of thirteen nominations. This remarkable achievement not only highlights the talent of Montpellier-based developer Sandfall Interactive but also marks a significant milestone for the French gaming industry, as it is the first time a French title has claimed the coveted Game of the Year award.

French President Emmanuel Macron took to social media to express his pride, celebrating the games success and the creativity it represents. With accolades including Best Indie Game, Best Art Direction, and Best Performance, Clair Obscur has proven to be a shining example of innovation and excellence in gaming. The game's success story is a testament to the hard work and dedication of its creators, who have captivated players worldwide.
